WHAT WE BELIEVE

live

Faith Fellowship Church is a Foursquare church which holds to the Articles of Faith of the International Church of the Foursquare Gospel (otherwise known as ICFG).

We believe in God the Father, God the Son, and God the Holy Spirit. This is otherwise known as the Trinity to which we hold.

We believe the Bible is God's authoritative Word to mankind and gives us all we need for right belief and right conduct.

We believe there is only one avenue to salvation, through Jesus Christ, God's only begotten Son, who was born of a virgin, lived a sinless life, died on the cross, was buried dead, whom God raised from the dead three days later, was seen by over 500 witnesses, ascended into heaven, is seated at God's right hand in authority over everything, and will return one day as King of kings and Lord of lords.

We believe in water baptism by complete submersion (if physically possible) for any one of any age who understands the simple gospel, that, all human beings are sinners and Jesus is the ONLY Savior, and that baptism comes after a person has confessed with their mouth that "Jesus is Lord", believing with their heart that God raised Him from the dead.

We believe in the Gifts of the Holy Spirit as listed in 1 Corinthians 12, which are still alive and active in the church today.

We believe in submitting to the laws of our land and to the governing authorities, unless those laws are a violation of God's Word.

We believe in praying for the President of our great country and all those in leadership positions whether we agree with them or not. We are still called to pray for them.

We believe in the personal holiness of each person who names the name of Jesus Christ.

We believe that Christians are to continue in regular church attendance to both grow spiritually, and also be involved in serving the local church body as much as is reasonably possible.

We believe in giving to God through the local church, by way of tithes and offerings.

We believe in training all saints who attend here to release them to use their individual gifts to serve the Lord as He deems fit.

We believe that God is the author of our Salvation and the finisher of our Faith.

This is what we believe.

FaithKidz

Our Mission Statement

FaithKIDZ Choir is dedicated to the development of Christian character through song in every child. All choir members have the opportunity to develop:

• A deeper relationship with our Savior

• A basic knowledge of choir vocal skills, execution of musical syncopation, and choral direction Christian leadership skills.

History

Victoria Williams Turner began the FaithKidz Choir in April 2010 and has done an amazing job with more than 45 choir members and parent leadership. The children are taught to praise God with a standard of excellence in their singing.

Accomplishments

The FaithKidz Choir has sung to overjoyed audiences at Faith Fellowship and in the surrounding area, including:

• 2010 Raise The Roof In Praise Concert

• 2011 United In Praise Concert

• 2015 San Leandro Mayor’s Christmas Celebration • 2017 Spring Concert • San Leandro Greenhouse Mall Safeway Christmas Celebration

Several FaithKIDZ Choir members have received advanced placement in their school choir as a direct result of participating in the FaithKidz Choir.

Academic and Spiritual Growth

Whenever available, an academic review is conducted on each song’s author and the reason they wrote the song.

FaithKidz Choir is a singing ministry providing service to the congregation of Faith Fellowship Worship Center up to six times per year and whenever called upon and available to minister. The members of the choir are encouraged to give service to their community and also have opportunities to serve in Sunday service as ushers, greeters, and sing on the worship team.

Once a year, the parents are encouraged to sing with their children; further strengthening the bonds of parenthood through Christ.

The families enjoy several field trips and activities that are fun, educational, and encourage long standing friendships with other choir members.

Koinonia

BACK TO DANCE

About Us

The Koinonia Praise Dance Ministry was formed several years ago by a small group of Christian women during an annual Women's Retreat at Faith Fellowship. The founding members shared a vision to minister to God's people and to draw others to Christ through dance and movement. Much like God's Kingdom, we are a diverse group with varying ethnicities, ages and spiritual journeys. Each member plays a special part, and we work together to maintain a unified ministry, while operating under a joint set of values.

We are often asked what the name Koinonia means. By definition, Koinonia means communion by intimate participation which is agreement with one another, being united in purpose, and serving alongside each other.

Our Mission

To teach and promote praise dancing as an acceptable act of worship in the church, as referenced in Psalm 149:3: "Let them praise his name with dancing and make music to him with tambourine and harp." Additionally, we seek to dance for God and to allow God to dance through us as a means of encouraging, building up, and leading His people in worship.

When the Lord has blessed you with feet to dance, leap and twirl with all your might. When your arms can express what is deep inside, let your heart reach out and shine. For God will use you, to express His love. There are people who need to see. Let Him use you to speak from your heart, through the dance. Such a gift God has made you to be.

Our Calling

To glorify God using His gift of dance; while incorporating prayer, worship, and Bible study. To minister to God's people, usher in a spirit of worship, intercede and break chains using choreographed and Spirit-led movement and fellowship with other Christians and support one another in love.

What We Do

Choreographed Worship

Seek to Worship the Lord with excellence through skillful choreographed dance.

Minister to the congregation while providing a visual message of God’s love, the gift of salvation, hope, encouragement and praise unto the Lord.

Use the gift of dance and the Holy Spirit to present purposeful dance movements from the heart; while using appropriate bodily and facial expressions that send a clear message.

Seek and utilize God’s spiritual gifts to dance with power, and to speak to the hearts of others in an effort to create thoughtful reflection of our Lord and our lives.

Spontaneous Worship (includes: Worship Prior to Rehearsal, Platform Ministry & Special Presentations)

Freely worship the Lord under His anointing through spontaneous individual dance.

Uplift, touch hearts and minister to the congregation through movement.

Become an active part of the overall Praise Team by working alongside the Tribe, Musicians and Choir to initiate and encourage a spirit of worship and praise within the congregation.

Interpret songs of praise and worship under the leading of the Holy Spirit through heart-felt movement, appropriate facial expression and body language.

Seek and utilize God’s spiritual gifting to dance as an act of warfare, intercession, and whenever possible prophetically in order to break chains of oppression.

Bible Study

A Bible study book will be chosen to read and review as a group. Each week a portion of the book will be selected. Each member is responsible for reading the selection and answering the related questions. Everyone will share their responses at the following Thursday’s rehearsal.

Member Requirements

Pray, ask and seek the Lord in order to determine that He has called you to this dance ministry.

Evaluate your current responsibilities to determine whether or not you can fully commit to the ministry at this time.

Commit your time and efforts to ensure the continued success and growth of the ministry.

Participate in a 6 week period of dedication: Upon joining the ministry each member will commit to a period of dedication. During this time of dedication it is important that you pray over your calling in the ministry. You will be responsible for attending all rehearsals and ministry events; however you must wait for a period of 6 weeks before ministering publicly.

Actively pursue the Lord and grow spiritually through regular Bible study and prayer; as well as periodic fasting both individually and as a group.

Be transparent and accountable to fellow members by participating in regularly scheduled discussions of spiritual books and discussions that pertain to the ministry.

Attend weekly rehearsals which are mandatory, practice on your own time, arrive on time, and arrive prepared for dances and other requirements.

Grow and enhance your dance abilities through regular participation in internal and external dance workshops, trainings and conferences.

Rehearsal

Thursday evenings from 6:30pm – 9:00pm in the NextGen Building

Pastor Vince Taylor

Senior Pastor

In June of 1998, I joined the staff of Faith Fellowship Pastors after serving 20 years in the U.S. Navy. At that time, I was also a Licensed Minister and Pastor in the Church of God denomination. My experience in the Foursquare denomination actually began in 1984, when my family and I were stationed at the Naval Hospital in Oakland. We then joined the church and always kept a special place in our hearts for this ministry, because God did so much in us during our stay in Oakland. In July of 1988, my family and I were transferred to Atsugi, Japan, where for three years we had the awesome opportunity to minister to many Japanese converts.

God has truly been good to me. He has given me a jewel and her name is Debra. For over 30 years, she has been by my side through every challenge, as well as every victory. We are the proud parents of our only son Lance, who is really a joy to us. Lance is a college graduate and has become quite a gentleman. We are also the proud grandparents of two, who we just love and adore.

We are presently enjoying a wonderful time of ministry here and hope that we are in some way a blessing to everyone who attends our services. God bless you and thank you.

Pastor Vincent Taylor Senior Pastor

A few of the translations we recommend: Interlinear Hebrew / Greek / English Bible NIV Study Bible (Large Print) NASB Study Bible Thomas Chain Reference Bible

Bible Concordance: helps you quickly find specific words or verses in Scripture, making it easier to study related passages. Strong's Exhaustive Concordance Compact Bible Concordance

Bible Lexicons: provide definitions and meaning of Biblical words found in the original New Testament Greek and Old Testament Hebrew languages of the Holy Bible. This study resource helps in understanding the origins and root meaning of the ancient language. Hebrew Lexicon Greek Lexicon

What We Believe: Brings clarity to our doctrinal beliefs. Foundations of Pentecostal Theology

Bible Commentary: helps clarify and explain Scripture, making it easier to understand and apply its teachings. Compact Bible Commentary

Bible Handbook: provides concise overviews and background information to help you understand the context and themes of Scripture. Holman Illustrated Bible Handbook Compact Bible Handbook

Bible Dictionary: helps you understand the meanings of biblical terms, names, and places for better comprehension of Scripture. Compact Bible Dictionary

WHILE WE'RE WAITING

Finding hope and life after the loss of a child can be incredibly challenging.

The loss of a child, no matter that child’s age, is profoundly life-altering. It’s as if the foundations of everything we once held dear have been shaken and cracked.

The While We’re Waiting support group is to point grieving mothers to our only true source of comfort, Jesus Christ. Although our topics of discussion may be painful, and tears will be inevitable, our desire will be to honor God by seeking how we can live well while we’re waiting to be reunited with our children some day.

An organization in relationship with Faith Fellowship.
